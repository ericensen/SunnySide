Camp Policies

Simple policies for a smooth camp day.

These policies are here to help families know what to expect before registration, on camp day, and if plans change.

Registration and Payment

Booking policies

Registration is first come, first served

Each camp day has limited space. A spot is considered reserved only after the registration form is submitted and payment is completed.

Sibling registrations

Families may register more than one child in the same checkout flow, as long as each child's name, age, and camp selections are included.

Payment timing

Please complete payment promptly after registration so your selected camp spots can be confirmed.

Refunds and Changes

If plans change

Cancellation requests

If you need to cancel, please contact SunnySide as soon as possible by email or phone.

Refunds

Refund requests made at least 7 days before the registered camp day will generally be honored in full. Requests made closer to camp may be handled as credits or partial refunds when possible.

Transfers

If space is available, SunnySide may allow a family to move a child to a different camp date instead of canceling outright.

Camp Day Expectations

Attendance, health, and pickup

Health and safety

Please do not send a child to camp if they are sick, have a fever, or have symptoms that would make participation unsafe for them or other campers.

Allergies and support needs

Families should share allergies, medical needs, or helpful comfort information during registration so staff can plan accordingly.

Pickup

Children will be released only to a parent, guardian, or authorized pickup contact listed by the family unless other arrangements have been confirmed.

Behavior and Participation

Creating a positive camp environment

Respectful behavior

SunnySide expects campers to treat other children, staff, supplies, and camp spaces with kindness and care.

Participation

Camp activities are designed to be playful and age-appropriate, but participation may vary based on each child's comfort level and needs.

Serious behavior concerns

If a child's behavior creates an ongoing safety issue or prevents the camp day from running smoothly, SunnySide may contact the family to discuss early pickup or future participation.
